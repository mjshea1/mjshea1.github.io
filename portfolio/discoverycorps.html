# Discovery Corps

Client: Pacific Science Center
Date: March 2018
Programs: Sketch, Zeplin, InVision
Duration: 3 Months

Description:
At the Pacific Science Center in Seattle, they inspire a lifelong interest in science, math and technology to more than 1 million people each year at its various campuses. For this project, the center teamed up with the University of Washington’s Information School to build a web application focused on recognizing student achievement through participation in the Discovery Corps science learning program. I was tasked as the lead UX and UI designer with a small team that included a Project Manager and a Developer.

# Objectives

The design goals were to provide the students the ability to:

  • Track learning progress and skill development through earning digital badges • Create and edit their own profiles
  • Create and curate an achievement portfolio to print or share digitally
  • View the progress of their peers to promote peer-supported learning
  • Message instructors about badge progress and event participation

# Designing Discovery Corps

The Discovery Corps web application was successfully deployed in early 2018. The Pacific Science Center has had great success supporting students from the time they enter the program until they graduate. Nearly 100% of the students who graduated from the program went on to apply to college.
